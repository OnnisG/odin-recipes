Dominican Cake

Crema de Leche

Crema de Leche Cake is a delightful dessert that features a light and fluffy sponge cake layered with rich, creamy milk-based frosting. The cake is typically soaked in a sweet milk syrup, adding moisture and flavor, and is often adorned with fresh fruit or chocolate shavings. Its luscious texture and sweet, subtle flavor make it a popular choice for celebrations and special occasions. Perfectly balanced, this cake is both comforting and indulgent, appealing to fans of creamy desserts.

In addition to its irresistible taste, Crema de Leche Cake is highly versatile, allowing for various flavor combinations and presentations. Bakers can experiment by incorporating different extracts, such as vanilla or almond, or by adding cocoa powder for a chocolate twist. The cake can be customized with seasonal fruits, such as berries, which not only enhance its visual appeal but also add a refreshing contrast to the richness of the frosting. Whether served at a festive gathering or enjoyed as a simple afternoon treat, Crema de Leche Cake is sure to impress and satisfy anyone with a sweet tooth. Its creamy layers and delightful sweetness make it a memorable dessert that leaves a lasting impression.

Ingredients

To prepare the pans

  • 2 tablespoon salted butter, at room temperature
  • 2 tablespoon all-purpose flour

For the cake

  • Pound all-purpose flour, (225 grams)
  • Tablespoons baking powder, (see notes)
  • pound salted butter, (225 grams), at room temperature
  • Pound sugar (white, granulated)
  • 225 grams egg yolk, plus enough egg white to weigh ½ lb (225 grams) in total, at room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon lime zest, freshly grated
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • ½ cup orange juice, at room temperature

Steps

Before starting

  • Preheat oven to 350 °F [175 °C], or 325 ºF [163 ºC] for convection ovens (see notes).
  • Mix the flour and baking powder and sift together. Divide into thirds and set aside.
  • Grease the baking pans, and lightly dust with flour. Set aside.
  1. Beating butter

Using the paddle attachment of your mixer, beat together butter and sugar at medium speed until the butter is light and fluffy and has a very light yellow color (about 4 mins).

  1. Adding eggs

Add the eggs a third at a time, and continue beating until each third is well incorporated into the mixture (about 2 mins) before adding the next third.Once eggs are added, with the mixer still running at mid-speed, add in vanilla and lime zest.

  1. Adding juice + flour

Increase speed to mid-high, and pour in a third of the juice, when it is well combined (about two mins) add a third of the flour and mix for another 2 mins. Pour in another third of the juice and mix for another 2 mins or until it is very well combined. Follow by adding another third of the flour, and mix for another 2 mins or until it is very well combined. Pour in the last third of the juice and mix for another 2 mins or until it is very well combined. Follow by adding the last third of the flour, and mix for another 2 mins or until it is very well combined.

  1. Filling the pans

As soon as you've stopped mixing the last batch of flour turn off the mixer, you should have obtained a fluffy batter with a smooth and even consistency. If necessary, scrape the sides and run for another minute to combine any unmixed batter that was stuck to the sides. Pour in half the batter in each baking pan, making sure not to disturb the butter/flour cover.

  1. Baking

Bake 30 minutes, or until a knife inserted in the center comes out clean (don't open the oven until after at least 30 mins). Cool to room temperature before removing from the pan. To assemble
